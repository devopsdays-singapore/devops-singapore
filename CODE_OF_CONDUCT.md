# Code of Conduct

DevOps Singapore is dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone attending our events and participating in our community (both online and offline).

### Expected Behavior
- Be kind and respectful to others.
- Be professional in all communications and interactions.
- Focus on constructive feedback and technical discussions.
- Allow everyone to participate and share their ideas in a safe environment.
- Be open to differing opinions and experiences.

### Unacceptable Behavior
The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- Harassment of any kind (including verbal, physical, or online)
- Insults, personal attacks, or derogatory comments
- Disruptive behavior during talks or discussions
- Sexual language or imagery
- Intimidation or bullying
- Any behavior that makes others feel unwelcome or uncomfortable

### Enforcement
Organizers and community leaders have the right to:
- Ask anyone engaging in unacceptable behavior to stop immediately.
- Warn, remove, or ban participants (temporarily or permanently) from events, GitHub discussions, or other community spaces.

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the organizers immediately. You can:
- Comment on the relevant event issue (if during an event)
- Open a new GitHub issue (marked as private if needed)
- Reach out directly to the organizers via Telegram or email

All reports will be handled confidentially and promptly.

### Scope
This Code of Conduct applies to all DevOps Singapore events, GitHub repositories, Telegram groups, and any situation where someone is representing the community.
